# 10-minute guided script segments (generic; thought shown on-screen)
# One segment per line. These will be generated to assets/audio/speech.

Welcome. You’ve already named the thought and rated how strong it feels. Nice work.
For this round, we’re not trying to argue with the thought or push it away. We’re practising noticing it.
As you play, let the thought sit quietly in the background. If it feels loud, that’s okay.
Do a gentle body check. Where do you feel it? Chest, throat, stomach, jaw, shoulders.
If you can, name the feeling that comes with it. Just one word is enough.
Try adding a little space: I’m having the thought that… and then add your thought on the end.
Now let it be there, and bring your attention back to the ball. Thought, body, ball.
Imagine a friend had this exact thought. What would you say to them? Keep it simple and kind.
Zoom out slightly. Is the thought pulling you to the past, the present, or the future?
If you want, hold one small balanced sentence alongside it. Like: This is a thought, not a fact.
Last check-in. Notice what’s changed, even a little. Take a slow breath.
